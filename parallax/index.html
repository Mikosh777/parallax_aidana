The Parallax Perspective

The motion of the Sun through space provides a longer baseline that will increase the accuracy of parallax measurements, known as secular parallax.

Other individual objects can have fundamental distance estimates made for them under special circumstances.

For example, if measuring the distance between two ticks on a line with a ruler marked on its top surface

Expansion parallaxes in particular can give fundamental distance estimates for objects that are very far, because supernova ejecta have large expansion velocities and large sizes (compared to stars). Further, they can be observed with radio interferometers which can measure very small angular motions

The distance estimate comes from computing how far the object must be to make its observed absolute velocity appear with the observed angular motion.
